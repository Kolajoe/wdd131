🌱 Green Energy DRC · Site Plan

Project: Green Energy in Democratic Republic of Congo — informational website with interactive CO₂ savings calculator.

📛 Site Name

🌿⚡ Green Energy DRC

Green Energy DRC

This name clearly communicates the geographic focus (Democratic Republic of Congo) and the core topic (renewable/green energy). It's memorable, search-friendly, and brandable. The name positions the site as an authoritative resource on DRC's energy transition.

Optional domain idea: greenenergydrc.org or congo-renewables.cd

🎯 Site Purpose

The website serves as an educational hub highlighting DR Congo's renewable energy potential (hydroelectric, solar, biomass). It provides:

  • Clear, accessible information on major initiatives (Inga Dam project, solar expansion, biomass resources).
  • Environmental and economic benefits of green energy adoption.
  • A JavaScript-based energy savings calculator showing estimated CO₂ reduction when switching from fossil fuels to renewables.
  • A "Get Involved" section with practical steps for readers to support or learn more.

The goal is to inspire awareness and action around sustainable energy in Central Africa.

💬 Scenarios

  • Scenario 1: "How much CO₂ could my neighborhood in Kinshasa save if we switched from diesel generators to solar power?"
    → The energy savings calculator answers this dynamically.
  • Scenario 2: "What is the current status of the Inga Dam project, and how would it benefit Central Africa?"
    → The Energy Sources page provides detailed, cited information.
  • Scenario 3: "I'm a student looking for renewable energy initiatives to support. Where do I start?"
    → The Get Involved page offers links, organizations, and advocacy tips.

📌 Implementation Notes

  • Interactive feature (JS): The CO₂ savings calculator will use an event listener (button or range slider) to compute estimated annual emissions saved (kg CO₂) based on user's current electricity usage (kWh) and local grid emission factor.
  • Responsive design: CSS Grid/Flexbox, viewport meta, media queries.
  • Form validation: ensure calculator inputs are positive numbers.
  • Accessibility: high color contrast (green/amber on light bg passes WCAG AA), alt text for images, semantic HTML.
  • Future adjustments: The final project may adapt wireframes slightly after development, but core layout remains as planned.
